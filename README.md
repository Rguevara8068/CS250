CS 250 README Reflection
How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating user stories help with this?

I interpret user needs by developing an understanding of what the customer wants out of the program and what it needs it to do and then breaking those needs into smaller tasks that are more managable. Creating user stories helps to do this because they focus on the user's perspective and clearly describe the features, the purpose, and the value it provides. During the SNHU Travel project, user stories made it simpler to organize development work and ensure that each feature met the customer's expectations.

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I have approached program development by notating needs,requirements, details needed for projects and completing them one step at a time. I have learned to ,ap everything down and create stepstones of what I want to accomplish and what requirements are needed. Throughout this course, I have seen that Agile development encourages continuous improvement through planning, testing, and feedback.In the future, I would like to use Agile practices like sprint planning, daily check-ins, sprint reviews, and retrospectives because they help teams stay organized, work together, and make changes when needed.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

What I have learned is being a good team member means communicating clearly, collaborating with others, and being willing to help solve problems that may arise. It also means listening to feedback, completing assigned work on time, and supporting the team's goals. During this course, I learned that successful software development depends on the teamwork just as much as any technical skills, and that working together helps produce better solid software for the customer.
